Inflo
The Problem:
Users do not have a centralized mechanism to research and view county statistics in order to make informed decisions.
The Solution:
Users should be able to view, slice and dice a centralized dashboard that aggregates statistical information from multiple county repositories.
The Benefits:
Users can make informed decisions regarding counties based on a dashboard that aggregates data from multiple environmental, demographic and natural hazard information repositories.
VISIT SITE
MEDCheck
The Problem:
Many prescription medications have serious side effects, causing "adverse effects" that are reported to the FDA. The average consumer does not have an easy mechanism to be aware of these issues.
The Solution:
Consumers should have an easy way to research prescription medications to see the side effects, reported adverse reactions, and any recalls.
The Benefits:
Consumer should be able to go to a single web site to research safety information of prescription medications and make informed choices on which prescription medication to choose based on history of adverse effects, label warnings, and recalls.
VISIT SITE
RefusalCheck
The Problem:
Consumers need a centralized mechanism to view statistical information on food and drug refusals.
The Solution:
Consumers should have a centralized dashboard to be able to view statistical information on food and drug refusals in order to identify food and drug related implications prior to consumption.
The Benefits:
Consumers will be able to search on manufacturer(s) or product(s) and obtain useful data including number of refusals, percentage of FDA sample analysis, country of origin and refusal date.
Lumify
The Problem:
Volumes of structured, unstructured, and semi-structured data at scale and at velocity, provide ingestion and dissemination challenges. This complicates efforts to analyze and graph relationships to analyze links.
The Solution:
Users can analyze relationships and automatically discovers paths between entities, and establishes new links.
The Benefits:
Users can ingest structured, unstructured, and semi-structured data from any source (including audio, image and video files), and can plot relationships either in a graph format or on a geospatial map.
