MEDCheck
The Problem:
Many prescription medications have serious side effects, causing "adverse effects" that are reported to the FDA. The average consumer does not have an easy mechanism to be aware of these issues.
The Solution:
Consumers should have an easy way to research prescription medications to see the side effects, reported adverse reactions, and any recalls.
The Benefits:
A solution that enables the average consumer to visit a portal to view medicinal information, their side effects and make informed decisions.

RefusalCheck
The Problem:
Consumers need a centralized mechanism to view statistical information on food and drug refusals.
The Solution:
Consumers should have a centralized dashboard to be able to view statistical information on food and drug refusals in order to identify food and drug related implications prior to consumption.
The Benefits:
Consumers will be able to search on manufacturer(s) or product(s) and obtain useful data including number of refusals, percentage of FDA sample analysis, country of origin and refusal date.

Air National Guard
The Problem:
The U.S. Air National Guard (ANG) Recruiting and Retention resources are constantly ensuring that the best and brightest are brought in to serve our country. ANG’s Recruiting and Retention personnel are distributed across 54 states and territories and to manage the complexities associated with such a wide-spread workforce, ANG had begun work on a platform to organize data and business intelligence dissemination and collection efforts. ANG realized that significantly more value could be gained by continuing to grow this platform.
The Solution:

Octo was engaged to provide end-to-end Agile Software Engineering services in support of an enterprise application that provides field personnel with the tools needed to effectively plan, execute, and learn from their recruiting and retention efforts.

Octo introduced a Scrum based agile software engineering process focused on project transparency and client collaboration to ensure that the software products meet the needs of ANG.

The Benefits:
In the first year of the program, Octo successfully released over 1,200 requirements to the production system. Existing planning modules were rebuilt to improve function, performance, and usability, and new modules were introduced that improve field users’ ability to build, and execute against strategic plans and budgets for recruiting and retention efforts. Because of the additional value Octo has added to the platform, user adoption by field recruiting and retention personnel is at an all-time high.

United States Army Corps of Engineers
The Problem:
The United States Army Corps of Engineers (USACE) builds and maintains America’s infrastructure and is responsible for the management of over $4.8 billion in projects. In order to ensure disciplined project management, USACE deployed the Promise2 (P2) system, which is fully integrated with the USACE financial management and contract management systems. The P2 system has not effectively adapted to the changing USACE requirements and shift to USACE overall strategy resulting in significant impact to user satisfaction and stakeholder objectives.
The Solution:

Octo has worked directly with the infrastructure management group at USACE ACE IT to prepare quality assurance standard operating procedures, provided a performance improvement plan for the P2 system that includes migration to Agile development principles and better alignment with functional stakeholders.

Octo established the P2 “Performance Improvement Program”, which has been the strategic outline and roadmap for the Program Manager to achieve improved performance, decreased sustainment cost and better alignment to functional requirements.

The Benefits:
The P2 system has reorganized under a single technical project management authority that provides daily technical oversight of the sustainment and operation of the system. Through the series of projects completed by Octo, USACE has identified gaps in processes and has established a more effective governance approach that has enabled the development and sustainment team to improve performance of the P2 System. The P2 System overall performance and executive advocacy has improved significantly.
